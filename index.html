About Us

Who are we?

Originating around DEF CON 22, DCZia is a group organized around the concepts of teaching, learning, and creating badges & other electronic hardware. Most of us have loose ties to New Mexico, but now we live all over the US. Proud holders of the DC DarkNet DEF CON 25 Black Badge!

Badges

Past and Present

2025 - DCZia Zippy Badge

Our love for retro tech and dead media formats never ends. Based on the Zip Disks of past, comes with 42 LEDs, an accelerometer, and a microphone. Blinks, and has a slick 3D Printed shell. Click of death not included.

2024 - DCZia Ziatron
Our original goal was a badge that would decode slow scan TV signals, but we were short on time, so we created an homage to the Sony Triniton TV with a badge that has a screen, leds, and a mic for sound reactive lighting. 3D Printed case diffueser, and dual PCB's will full color dye sublimation design.
2023 - DCZia Electric Sampler
This year we wanted to make something that would function as more than just a badge, and have use after DefCon. Thus the "Electric Sampler" was born, a eurorack format badge that has a variety of fun light modes, but also is a sequencer, sampler, midi controler, and more!
2022 - DCZia Electronics - 30 in One Badge
A throwback to the elctronic kits that taught many of us how circuits and componets can create awesome things
2022 - ZiaCon 3 "Hal9000 Badge"
Designed by Lithochasm for DCZia's annual get together Ziacon 3
2021 - ZiaCon 2 "YoloBadge"
Hamster made a save icon into a badge live during his 2022 Ziacon 2 talk
2020 - PiBadge Mini + XL
A build it at home badge for the first "Canceled" Defcon
2019 - Laser Theramin Synth Badge
A theramin inspired synth badge that uses laser rangefinders to make sound
2018 - KeyGrid Badge
Dczia got into mechanical keyboards, and it inspired a badge
2017 - Key Badge
An early attempt at PCB development; Designed as a shield to plug into an Arduino
2015 and 2016 - Pi Badge MkI and MkII
Our first badges. Off the shelf parts, custom software to show some pretty opensource Beeple and in house designed 3D visuals
